gatito's 2022 top 10



this list is still a work in progress seeing as how the year isnt over. keeping this in mind, here are the top 10 works of literature i have read this year so far [click on title to go to full review]

  1. The Kings Men - Nora Sakavic | (10/10)

    read: february | let me just say that this is not a good book at all.... despite all of its flaws, i have named this book my favorite book of the year. its honestly so dramatic and insane and unpredictable, but it's also addicting and shocking and amazing and i love it [book 3 of the all for the game trilogy]

    tags: found-family, mafia-drama, b-plot-queer-romance, please-check-warnings, amazing-romance-tho

  2. The Raven King - Nora Sakavic | (10/10)

    read: february | this book man,,, especially after that first half?!! oh my god, the emotion! also!! that ENDING!!! probably the best part of the entire book, the way it was presented?! amazing. the execution: *chefs kiss*. the moment i put the book down i stared at my ceiling for a couple minutes. [book 2 of the all for the game triology]

    tags: mafia-drama, found-family, c-plot-romance, omg-what-is-going-on, THE-ENDING!!

  3. The Raven Boys - Maggie Stiefvater | (10/10)

    read: march | the plot was incredibly confusing and i found myself just trying to feel my way through most of the time, but the one thing that hooked me in was the characters and the way they're presented to the reader with such distinct and incredible personalities. [book 1 of the raven cycle series]

    tags: urban-fantasy, found-family, boarding-school-kids, confusing-plot-but-thats-not-the-important-part, amazing-cast-of-characters

  4. The Raven King - Maggie Stiefvater | (10/10)

    read: april | all i can really say about this book is that these characters evoke such a strong emotion in me that it cannot physically be explained. the character dynamics are written beautifully and it was honeslty such a great ending for the series. [book 4 of the raven cycle series]

    tags: urban-fantasy, found-family, b-plot-romances, confusing-plot-but-thats-not-the-important-part, amazing-cast-of-characters

  5. Choices (fanfiction) - messermoon | (10/10)

    read: may | two words: emotional devastation. did i come into 2022 thinking i would be shipping james potter and regulus black? no i did not. i didnt even go into this fic thinking about jegulus, i went in for the wolfstar content!,, and yet here i am emotionally devastated. 100% recommend.

    tags: marauders-fanfiction, romance, drama, amazing-writing-honestly-only-fic-writers-can-do-this, wolfstar, jegulus, horribly-sad

  6. Chain of Iron - Cassandra Clare | (8.8/10)

    read: january | whatever cassandra clare writes, i will most certainly read it. and i will love it. something about the way cassandra clare writes romance is so appealing to me. i eat it up, every. single. time. and the ending! for this.. on my knees, i NEEDED the next installation asap and yet i found out we had to wait until 2023!! insane. [book 2 of the last hours trilogy]

    tags: fantasy, shadowhunter-universe, b-plot-romance, adventure, found-family, amazing-cast-of-characters, THE-ENDING

  7. The Final Empire - Brandon Sanderson | (9.5/10)

    read: april | this book was my introduction to high fantasy and it did not disappoint! the plot was a heist, which meant that the book was going to be amazing. (dont ask me those are just facts). also VIN! i loved her so much. the magic system was incredibly complex and yet simple to understand. amazing worldbuilding. [book 1 of the mistborn trilogy]

    tags: epic-fantasy, heist-story, complex-world/magic-system, thought-provoking, easy-to-read, no-romance!

  8. The Dream Thieves - Maggie Stiefvater | (10/10)

    read: march | this book revolved around my favorite character in this series: mister ronan lynch. also gansey and blue! usually the middle books in series tend to be not as good since they focus on fleshing out the characters. however! i LOVE that! thats always been my favorite part in books. [book 2 of the raven cycle series]

    tags: urban-fantasy, laying-romance-framework, found-family, character-heavy-book, confusing-plot

  9. The Foxhole Court - Nora Sakavic | (9/10)

    read: february | i went in completely blind i think that is how everyone should approach these books. they are 100% more enjoyable when you don't know a single thing about these. while this is my least favorite of the trio. as of june 2022, i have read this book 3 times. so yeah. [book 1 of the all for the game trilogy]

    tags: mafia-drama, sports?, laying-groundwork, found-family, im-sorry-they-all-have-problems, neil<3

  10. Blue Lily, Lily Blue - Maggie Stiefvater | (10/10)

    read: march | another fleshing out characters book. aka why i loved it so much! i love character centric books with all my heart. ronan and adam and gansey and blue and noah, i love them so much, words are not enough. [book 3 of the raven cycle series]

    tags: urban-fantasy, c-plot-romance?, found-family, character-heavy-book, confusing-plot

gatito's 2022 honorable mentions



this list is meant for the books i rather enjoyed/had fun with. they're not necessarily good books, but they kept me entertained [click on the title to go to full review]

  1. City of Heavenly Fire - Cassandra Clare | (10/10)

    read: march | the first time i read this book was around 8 years ago (i was 13? 14?). i had read all of the previous books in the series and i spent a year waiting for this book. it did not disappoint. rereading it the second time around gave me the same rush it gave me the first time and that makes me incredibly happy. the other books in the series aren't as great but this one holds a special place in my heart. [book 6 of the mortal instruments series]

    tags: fantasy, shadowhunter-universe, romance, adventure, nostalgic

  2. The Hero of Ages - Brandon Sanderson | (8.5/10)

    read: april | by this point in the trilogy i was fully invested in these characters. spook and tensoon and vin were probably my favorite characters out of the entire cast and i found myself looking forward to their chapters. while the pacing for this book was better than the second installment, nothing beats the first book in my opinion. [book 3 of the mistborn trilogy]

    tags: epic-fantasy, moral-dilemas, multiple-povs, no-romance-plot, still-thinking-about-that-ending, spook<3

  3. Lady Midnight - Cassandra Clare | (9.5/10)

    read: may | this was also a reread. i love this book for many reasons but specifially because of the blackthorn family. they have probably the saddest backstory out of all the cassandra clare characters, and its crazy because we got to experience it in the other series, and then we get their series and we find out that they're actuallly not okay! and its so sad but interesting to read about. [book 1 of the dark artifices trilogy]

    tags: fantasy, romance, adventure, shadowhunter-universe, actually-incredibly-dramatic, the-family<3<3

  4. Tuck Everlasting - Natalie Babbit | (8.8/10)

    read: june | this was a very nostalgic read. i read this book in fifth grade and it was a fun little book for me to read. now, i find it a little bittersweet. it is a sad story but its also very happy and very coming of age-esque. only around 100 pages, so quick read.

    tags: urban-fantasy, childrens-book, whimsical, fun-quick-read, nostalgic-for-me<3

  5. Vicious - V.E Schwab | (8.5/10)

    read: may | victor was definitely my favorite character, i love his grey area of morality and how we are supposed to believe that hes the villain (when he isnt). the book has a heist vibe to it and it also has a jumpy timeline. enjoyable read but i wouldnt recommend it often. [book 1 of the vicious duology]

    tags: urban-fantasy, morally-grey-main-character, found-family?, enjoyable-first-book, horrible-second-book<3

  6. City of Lost Souls- Cassandra Clare | (8.8/10)

    read: march | another shadowhutner universe book? yeah. im rereading them this year so expect a lot of these. this was the first good book in this series and this was the second to last book so that says a lot. i just love these characters a lot so im extremely biased. [book 5 of the mortal instruments series]

    tags: fantasy, shadowhunter-universe, romance, emotional?!, adventure, the-characters!!<3

  7. Seven Deaths of Evelyn Hardcastle - Stuart Turton | (7.5/10)

    read: june | this was a confusing yet entertaining and refreshing read. i dont tend to read these types of books so when i do, its a nice little break from my norm. the timeline was all wacky and jumbled and the ending was bad, but overall enjoyable. i finished it in a day, so that means something.

    tags: dystopian-sci-fi, confusing-plot-but-you-get-there-eventually, bad-ending, wonky-timeline?
